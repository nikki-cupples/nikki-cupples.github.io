Te Whare Tapa Whā
September 16, 2024

Encompassing four key dimensions grounded on a foundation of being connected to the land (Whenua), Tā Mason Durie's 1984 Te Whare Tapa Whā models personal wellbeing. The first segment, taha tinana, focuses on our physical wellbeing and how we interact and take care of our bodies on a daily basis. Taha hinengaro, mental wellbeing, centralizes around, "how you feel, as well as how you communicate and think," including stress responses. Next, spritual wellbeing or taha wairua navigates each individual's belief system, whether that is religion, faith or a sense of meaning and purpose. Finally, taha whānau (family wellbeing) includes relationships and quality time spent with immediate family, friendships, and those we choose to surround ourselves with.

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

Exercise for at least an hour everyday. Continue attending aerials classes 3x per week. Try to start running every second morning, work my way up to everyday. Prepare lunch the night before to ensure healthy eating.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

Do something with a friend at least once a week, committing time to seeing them. Check in with my friends that I haven't heard from in a while. Walk my little sister to school at least once a week. Have a family dinner at least 2x a week with my parents and little sister.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Meditate every morning before starting my Dev Academy work. Practice listening & looping to be more present in my conversations. Ask myself two questions in the morning and two in the evening to center mental focus. Try journalling for 10 minutes before I go to sleep.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Try to do yoga at least 2x a week to connect with myself. Practice visualisation exercises for 5 minutes before entering a new environment. Practice breathing exercises when I am stressed. Create a board with printed photos of my goals and ambitions.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Walk to my aerials classes rather than taking the bus. Have lunch and my morning coffee outside everyday. Test out walking in a new area in my neighbourhood every weekend. Go to the beach more!
